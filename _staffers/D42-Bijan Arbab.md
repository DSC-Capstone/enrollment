description: |
  Three Projects: (1) Investigate the factors that delay permitting in the City of San Diego, (2) Image analyzer for non-emergency citizen service requests, (3) Monitor County Weather Station Data in Real-time for flood water and other emergency reports.

  Learning to investigate, earn trust, and discover what problems and issues need to be addressed in order to improve organizational issues is a focus of this project as much as determining and learning technical skills required for the analysis to discover and communicate actionable insights from the provided data for the City and County of San Diego.

  The Development Services Department (DSD) in the City of San Diego handles all the building permits from plumbing system repairs to new housing developments. However, the permit approval process isn't as efficient as it could be. Perhaps DSD needs to hire more electrical engineers as there aren't enough to handle all the permits that involve electrical work, or there are seasonal effects at play. This project will be a deep data dive utilizing machine learning to identify what factors delay the permitting process, and the results can have a big impact in changing the business process for the City of San Diego.

  The City of San Diego's Get It Done app receives over 1000 requests each day from residents in need of city services from potholes to graffiti removal, short-term rental violations, and more. Many of the requests have images included. Currently, the images aren't utilized to their full potential. This project will be to create an image analyzer that can classify case types for more accurate reporting, pull image metadata to get more accurate location data, identify more enhanced case details (such as derogatory graffiti which has a higher priority), and more. This system can reduce workloads for City staff, identify higher priority issues, and improve the quality of service from the City of San Diego.
