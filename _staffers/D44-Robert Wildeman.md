bio: |
  Robert Wildeman is an industry partner at Bosch Home Comfort, working on AI-assisted tools for field technicians and contractors who service Bosch products.

description: |
  Many use-cases for AI-assisted tools deal with dynamic, modern systems that can be patched, updated, or architected with AI in mind; however, many of the critical systems and objects in our daily lives exist fully outside of this sphere of connectivity. In order to provide long-term support for their products, Bosch Home Comfort needs to guide contractors and field technicians to the correct replacement parts for the units they are servicing — not only in a database, but in the real world.

  An effective AI-assistant will need to provide an interface that is multi-lingual, familiar with industry terminology, and appealing to technicians, while also ensuring that all of its answers are correct, up-to-date, and authoritatively sourced. Using sales data, usage analytics, warranty claim trends, and other unstructured inputs, the agent should learn to guide technicians to the parts relevant to the repairs currently being performed.

  Unlike a typical use-case that ends when the session does, this use-case is concluded only when the technician has the required part in-hand. The solution to this problem may involve calling an API endpoint for incoming orders, or Jimmy in the warehouse — both scenarios are likely to be in-play here. Accomplishing this task will require creative problem-solving, frequent user-feedback sessions, and a willingness to quickly pivot when needed.

summer: |
  We'll want to at least have students confirm that they have access to any accounts or environments that we set up on our side, so that we can have them up and running day-one. We can also provide some high-level industry information and maybe have them start looking at the market more broadly so that they're not coming in blind on the subject. We'll do our best to provide a high-level architecture in the coming months so that we're working with tools and services allowed inside of Bosch.

  We have all of the starting data. A good goal would be to produce a functional proof-of-concept by the end of fall quarter so that we can work with usage data in the spring.

goal: |

The usage data that we collect from those users, and the internal feedback that we get from Bosch stakeholders will help drive our approach during the spring quarter.
